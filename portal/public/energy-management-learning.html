Core Concepts

What ISO 50001 Means

ISO 50001 is the international standard for an Energy Management System. It gives an organization a structured way to monitor energy use, set objectives, define responsibilities, review results, and improve energy performance over time.

What An Energy Baseline Means

An energy baseline is the reference point used to compare future performance. In practice, it is the expected level of energy use for a machine or process under normal operating conditions, so deviations, savings, and losses can be measured consistently.

What An EnPI Means

An Energy Performance Indicator, or EnPI, is a measurable value used to track energy performance. In HumanEnerDIA, EnPIs help compare actual energy use against the baseline and show whether performance is improving, stable, or drifting.

What A Significant Energy Use Means

A Significant Energy Use, or SEU, is a machine, process, or system that consumes a large amount of energy or has strong improvement potential. The pilot focuses analysis and recommendations on these high-impact areas.

How HumanEnerDIA Applies These Concepts

HumanEnerDIA connects ISO 50001 concepts to factory work through monitoring, analysis, and documentation. The Baseline page supports expected-versus-actual analysis, the Reports page supports management review, and the Pilot Procedures page supports the operational response to anomalies and efficiency issues.
