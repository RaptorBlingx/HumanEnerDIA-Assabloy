Pilot Procedures

This page provides the operational guidance used in the simulated Romanian pilot. It supports the documentation tasks in the benchmark and connects HumanEnerDIA outputs to practical factory procedures.

Simulated Pilot Context

The guidance below is written for the Simulated Romanian Pilot Factory and the Romanian Auxiliary Production Facility used in the A/B benchmark recordings.

Energy Policy Summary

  • The facility commits to continual improvement in energy performance.
  • Energy objectives and targets must align with production, quality, safety, and equipment reliability.
  • Energy data must be reviewed regularly and used to support maintenance, scheduling, and reporting decisions.
  • Energy-efficient purchasing, operational planning, and corrective action are required when performance deviates from baseline.
  • Relevant personnel must know the approved policy, procedures, and the consequences of departing from them.

Anomaly Response Procedure

  1. Review the anomaly context: affected machine, time window, severity, and deviation from expected performance.
  2. Confirm whether it is operational, maintenance-related, or data-quality related.
  3. Check current machine status and recent production context before escalation.
  4. If the issue is minor and explainable, log it for monitoring and continue observation.
  5. If the issue indicates excessive consumption, abnormal operation, or repeated recurrence, notify the energy or maintenance engineer.
  6. Record the finding and the action taken in the reporting or improvement workflow.

Energy Efficiency Response Procedure

  1. Compare actual performance against baseline or expected performance.
  2. Identify the machine or process causing the largest deviation or cost impact.
  3. Review recent anomalies, uptime pattern, and production context.
  4. Decide whether the issue is caused by scheduling, operating practice, maintenance condition, or load pattern.
  5. Capture at least one recommended action: adjust scheduling, inspect equipment, verify control settings, or monitor recurrence.
  6. Include the finding in the relevant KPI or monthly report.

Monthly Reporting Procedure

  1. Select the target factory, year, and month on the Reports page.
  2. Generate the monthly energy report.
  3. Review the executive summary, energy overview, machine profiles, cost analysis, and carbon analysis.
  4. Summarize the major consumers, deviations or savings, KPI or EnPI status, and follow-up actions.
  5. Share the report output with the operational and technical stakeholders.

Roles And Responsibilities

Operational UserFacility / Production Manager

Uses overview, documentation, and reports for operational decisions and confirms whether findings require production-side action.

Technical UserEnergy / Maintenance / Automation Engineer

Validates anomalies and deviations, interprets deeper analysis results, and recommends corrective or optimization actions.

Management ReviewReviewer

Reviews KPI trends, EnPI status, and action outcomes during the management review cycle.
