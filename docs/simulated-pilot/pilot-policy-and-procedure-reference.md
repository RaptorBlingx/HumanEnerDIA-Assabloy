# Pilot Policy And Procedure Reference

This file is the source content for the simulated pilot's documentation tasks.

Use it in two ways:
- as the manual reference source for `Condition A`
- as the content source to load or mirror into the chatbot knowledge base for `Condition B`

## 1. Energy Policy Summary
- The facility is committed to continual improvement in energy performance.
- Energy objectives and targets must align with production needs, quality, worker safety, and equipment reliability.
- Energy data must be reviewed regularly and used to support decisions on maintenance, scheduling, and reporting.
- The facility supports energy-efficient purchasing, operational planning, and corrective action where performance deviates from baseline.
- Relevant personnel must be aware of the energy policy, applicable procedures, and the consequences of departing from approved practice.

## 2. Anomaly Response Procedure
1. Review the anomaly context:
   - affected machine
   - timestamp or time window
   - severity
   - deviation from expected performance
2. Confirm whether the anomaly is operational, maintenance-related, or data-quality related.
3. Check current machine status and recent production context before deciding on escalation.
4. If the issue is minor and explainable, log it for monitoring and continue observation.
5. If the issue indicates excessive consumption, abnormal operation, or repeated recurrence, notify the energy / maintenance engineer.
6. Record the finding and any action taken in the improvement or reporting workflow.

## 3. Energy Efficiency Response Procedure
1. Compare actual performance against baseline or expected performance.
2. Identify the machine or process creating the largest deviation or cost impact.
3. Review recent anomalies, uptime pattern, and production context.
4. Decide whether the issue is caused by scheduling, operating practice, maintenance condition, or load pattern.
5. Capture at least one recommended action:
   - adjust scheduling
   - inspect equipment condition
   - verify control settings
   - monitor for recurrence
6. Include the finding in the relevant KPI or monthly report.

## 4. Monthly Reporting Procedure
1. Select the target factory, year, and month.
2. Generate the monthly energy report from the Reports page.
3. Review report sections relevant to the case:
   - executive summary
   - energy overview
   - machine profiles
   - cost analysis
   - carbon analysis
4. Summarize the main findings:
   - major consumers
   - deviations or savings
   - KPI or EnPI status
   - recommended follow-up actions
5. Share the report output with the relevant operational and technical stakeholders.

## 5. Roles And Responsibilities
- `Facility / production manager`
  - uses overview, documentation, and report outputs for operational decisions
  - confirms whether findings require production-side action
- `Energy / maintenance / automation engineer`
  - validates anomalies and performance deviations
  - interprets deeper analysis results
  - recommends corrective and optimization actions
- `Management reviewer`
  - reviews KPI trends, EnPI status, and action outcomes
